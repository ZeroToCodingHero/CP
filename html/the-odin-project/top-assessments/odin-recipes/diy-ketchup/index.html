What is Ketchup?

Ketchup is a tangy, sweet sauce made from tomatoes, vinegar, sugar, and various spices. It's a staple condiment for many dishes, especially burgers and fries.

Prep Time: 5 minutes

Yield: ≈ 1 cup

Storage: Refrigerate up to 2 weeks

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (6 ounces) tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the tomato paste, apple cider vinegar, honey (or maple syrup), onion powder, garlic powder, salt, black pepper, and smoked paprika (if using) until smooth.
  2. Adjust the seasoning to taste. If you prefer a sweeter ketchup, add more honey or maple syrup. For a tangier flavor, add more vinegar.
  3. Transfer the ketchup to a clean jar or bottle and refrigerate for at least 1 hour before using to allow the flavors to meld together.
  4. Enjoy your homemade ketchup on burgers, fries, or as a dipping sauce!

Tips for the Best Homemade Ketchup

  • Use high-quality tomato paste for a richer flavor.
  • Feel free to experiment with additional spices like cinnamon, cloves, or cayenne pepper for a unique twist.
  • Store the ketchup in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

Enjoy your homemade ketchup! 🍅
