DIY Homemade Mayonnaise (1-Minute Stick Blender Method)

Creamy, fresh, and paleo-friendly mayonnaise made in just one minute with an immersion blender. Once you try homemade, you'll never go back to store-bought!

Prep Time: 1 minute

Yield: ≈ 1 cup

Storage: Refrigerate up to 4–5 days

Ingredients

  • 1 large egg (room temperature is best)
  • ½ tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon white wine vinegar (or apple cider vinegar)
  • ¼ teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • ¼ teaspoon sea salt (or to taste)
  • 1 cup avocado oil (recommended) or light-flavored olive oil

Instructions (Stick Blender Method)

  1. Add the egg, lemon juice, vinegar, Dijon mustard, and salt into a tall jar or the container that came with your immersion blender.
  2. Slowly pour the oil on top. Let the ingredients settle for 30–60 seconds — do not stir.
  3. Insert the stick blender all the way to the bottom of the jar.
  4. Turn it on and keep it pressed firmly at the bottom for 10–15 seconds until the bottom turns thick and creamy white.
  5. Slowly move the blender up and down until all the oil is emulsified and the mayonnaise is thick and fluffy (total blending time ≈ 30–45 seconds).
  6. Taste and adjust salt or lemon juice if needed.
  7. Store in a clean jar in the refrigerator.

Tips for Success

  • Use a tall, narrow container so the blender fits snugly at the bottom.
  • Avocado oil gives the most neutral taste. Avoid extra-virgin olive oil if you don’t want a strong flavor.
  • The egg can be cold, but room temperature ingredients emulsify more easily.
  • If the mayo breaks, you can usually rescue it by adding another egg yolk to a new jar and slowly blending the broken mixture into it.

Enjoy your fresh homemade mayonnaise! 🥪
